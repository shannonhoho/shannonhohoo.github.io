Information:

As some of you may know this already, the whole Korean wedding industry is built around 'speed and convenience.'

When you first arrive at the venue, you will see Seho greeting guests with both parents in the lobby. You will then be guided to the "bride room” to chat and take photos with Erica.

The actual ceremony will take no longer than half-hour-- bridesmaids, flower girls, ring bearer, and groomsmen will be there. There is also a speech, a brief musical performance, the kiss, and...many many awkward wedding pictures. And that’s about it.

After the 'standard' wedding ceremony is a short traditional Korean ceremony called Pyebaek, where Erica and Seho will wear special wedding Hanbok and will bow to their older family members who are seated behind a low table stocked with traditional and symbolic wedding foods such as chestnuts, jujubes, and dried persimmons. The 'adults' will bless the newlywed couple, and there will be an entertaining round of “catch the jujubes and chestnuts” to predict how many daughters and sons we will have. You will see Erica trying her best not to catch any of them.

Everyone will then walk over to the buffet. There's no special reception program(s) so guests are expected to eat their meals, greet the happy couple in their regular Hanbok and leave.

On whole, you can expect to leave the wedding within 3-4 hours after arrival.
